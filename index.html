About us

Starling Ventures, LLC is an investment vehicle for a small group of current and former financial professionals established in 2011. It has invested in more than 70 early or growth-stage technology ventures and a number of privately placed funds in biotech.

Chi-fu Huang

Member, Management Committee

Chi-fu Huang is a private investor, a retired hedge fund manager, and a former finance academic. He is a Board Member of DeepMacro, LLC, a Board Member of Sycamore Investment Services, Inc., and an owner of ConvexityWines.com. He serves on Corporate Development Committee and Advisory Council of Dean of School of Sciences, Massachusetts Institute of Technology. 

In 1999, Chi-fu co-founded, with Myron Scholes, Platinum Grove Asset Management L.P. (PGAM). He was the CEO and CIO from 1999 to 2009, and served as Non-Executive Chairman from 2010 to 2013.  

Before founding PGAM, Chi-fu was with Long Term Capital Management L.P. from 1995 to 1999 and was a Partner and Co-Head of its Asia office in Tokyo from 1997 to 1999. Prior to that he was Head of Fixed Income Derivatives Research at Goldman, Sachs & Co. in New York from 1993 to 1994.  

Chi-fu received his B.A. in Economics from National Taiwan University in 1977 and Ph.D. in Financial economics from Stanford University in the United States in 1983. He was on the faculty of the MIT Sloan School of Management from 1983 to 1994, and was a full professor holding the J.C. Penney Professorship in Finance when he left MIT in the summer of 1994.  

John W. Meriwether

Member, Management Committee

John W. Meriwether is Principal and founder of JM Advisors, LLC (“JMA”), an investment management firm based in Greenwich, Connecticut. Mr. Meriwether is the Chief Executive Officer of JMA and in that capacity is active in all aspects of the firm’s business including trading, new investment and general management. He is an owner of ConvexityWines.com.  

Mr. Meriwether began his career with Salomon Brothers in 1974 and was named Vice President in 1977. In 1978, Mr. Meriwether was selected to start the Domestic Fixed Income Arbitrage Group. He was admitted as a General Partner of Salomon Brothers in 1980 and was appointed to the firm’s Board of Directors in 1986. Mr. Meriwether was appointed Vice Chairman of Salomon Brothers in February 1988, responsible for worldwide Fixed Income Trading, Fixed Income Arbitrage and Foreign Exchange Trading. Mr. Meriwether left Salomon Brothers in 1991.  

He formed Long-Term Capital Management, L.P. in early 1993, and JWM Partners, LLC in 1999; he was a Principal and CEO of both firms. In addition to his other business activities, Mr. Meriwether was a Director of the Chicago Board of Trade from 1985 to 1990.  

Mr. Meriwether was born in Chicago, Illinois in 1947. He received his B.S. degree from Northwestern University in 1969 and his M.B.A. degree from the Graduate School of Business of the University of Chicago in 1973.
